Date Notes
01/12/2020 P-3 check flight. Calibrations maneuvers for TAMMS. Some cloud penetrations.
01/15/2020 ER-2 transit flight. Some overflights of thick cloud and light precip.
01/18/2020 Planned N-S mesoscale stacked bowties with GPM overpass at 1858UTC. ER-2 forced to abort due to mechanical issue very early into flight. P-3 completed 3 bowtie patterns and overflew SBU instrumentation. UIUC launched 5 sondes. SBU launched 4. A total of 9 supplementary sondes were launched from KBUF, KALB and KGYX.
01/25/2020 3 stacked bowties that followed the storm and sampled convective bands ahead of an occluded front. AMPR data in second half of flight is limited and Nevzorov probe had a failure ~2300UTC. P-3 launched 2 dropsondes and there were 5 mobile rawinsonde launched (UIUC-4, SBU-1).
02/01/2020 Racetracks sampled developing offshore cyclone. Stratiform rain with relatively shallow tops (-10 to -15C) were sampled with some embedded elevated convective bands during GPM underflight. CRS lost about 10 minutes of data around 14 UTC. Nevzorov was not operational for this flight and one of the HVPS probes was noisy. 4 dropsondes were released from aobut 25000 ft during the first racetrack leg and 4 more were released during the satellite underflight.
02/05/2020 SBU did operate ground radars, but not in coordination with aircraft which were working a different storm. Elevated convection was seen to the south, mulitband structures seen to the north. *PHIPS did not collect data due to a dead battery which was not discovered until after the flight
02/07/2020 SBU observations not relevant to aircraft obs located farther north and west. Precipitation from a mix of frontal uplift, generating cells. GOES shows significant gravity wave activity.
02/13/2020 ER-2 could not fly because airfield was closed. A lot of gravity waves to south with multi-bands and waves to the north with orographics.
02/18/2020 ER-2 could not fly due to an electrical issue. Sampled the warm sector of a low-pressure system with P-3, but elevated convection or wave features were not likely sampled given the observations onboard the P-3.
02/20/2020 ER-2 could not fly due to high wind forecast. Sampled the NW sector of a low-pressure system with P-3 over NC and observed elevated convection and wave features associated with snow bands that dumped 1-5 inches over northern NC and southern VA. Sharp frontal boundary between snowing region to north and rain south.
